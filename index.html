The Air Disaster Memorial


THE BURNING AIR MAN


ABOUT:

1940. Canberra’s worst air disaster. When a Lockheed Hudson bomber plummeted into a hillside near Queanbeyan, burst into flames and killed all 10 people on board instantly. The fatalities included several members of Robert Menzies’ ministry who were flying to Canberra for an important war cabinet meeting. It was a disaster from which Menzies’ United Australia Party government never managed to recover.

THE HAUNTING:

For several decades the lonely crash site, now surrounded by pine forest, has been regarded as the most haunted location in Canberra and one which generations of locals have been dared to explore at night ... alone. On the twenty-year anniversary of the crash, a monument was erected at the site by the previous Prime Minister, Robert Menzies. It’s not clear if the reports of the spectral burning airman happened before the memorial was erected. The area became known as a popular make-out spot before the road leading to the memorial was closed.

SCARE FACTOR:

High - stories of people running from the forest in the dark of the night vowing never to return doesn't exactly instil a high level of comfort in those planning to visit.



STRANGE OCCURANCES:



This legend has various versions, but the most common one is: If you stop your car on the cattle guard, a nurse who was on the scene at the time of the crash helping the wounded will run towards your car and try to get in. There have been several sightings– like the teenager who left the forest screaming claiming she was being followed by images of an airman on fire and the young man who said that he had seen a ghost-like figure dart across the road in front of him. Stories of whispering voices and running footsteps, strange lights, and, photos containing numerous orbs. There are also tales of people claiming they hear the noise of a phantom plane crashing. As if that’s not enough to send shivers racing up your spine, this forest also has a close link to one of Canberra’s unsolved mysteries – the murder of Keren Rowland, whose body was found near the Air Disaster Memorial on May 13, 1971. To this day, almost 50 years later, her death remains unsolved.

Blundells Cottage


GHOST OF FLORA


ABOUT:

This cottage next to Lake Burley Griffin is a classic for locals when it comes to haunted spots around Canberra. Built-in 1860, Blundell’s Cottage was home to the Ginn family, then later passed onto George Blundell and his family. The cottage had multiple other residents, but coined the name Blundell since the family lived in there the longest; 59 years from 1874-1933. The paranormal presence that many visitors seem to encounter at Blundell’s is believed to be that of the eldest Blundell child, Flora.

THE HAUNTING:

The apparition of a teenage girl playing in the English gardens around this historic rubble-stone relic from the mid-1800s is thought by paranormal investigators to be the ghost of Florrie Blundell, who died in 1892 at the age of 16 as a result of burns sustained in an ironing accident. When she lived there she got too close to the fireplace and accidentally caught fire.

SCARE FACTOR:

low.The ghost of Florrie has never harmed anyone. In fact, some psychic mediums claim her ghost enjoys visitors, especially those of a similar age and gender (teenage girls).



STRANGE OCCURANCES


Visitors have sworn to hear her ‘scream’ accompanied with a scent of burning. The staff that man the little museum talk about odd things that happen in cottage, including items being mysteriously moved overnight to unexplained feelings of someone watching you and even more unnerving, the smell of burning human flesh. Bizarrely it is said that only people wearing a necklace can feel the presence of this spirit. And the reason for this? It is said that Flora was wearing a necklace when she died.

Hotel Kurrajong


THE GHOST OF CHIFLEY


ABOUT:

Hotel Kurrajong Canberra’s corridors are steeped in the capital’s political history. The hotel team consider themselves proud custodians of its heritage, passionately sharing stories of its early days during guided tours. The most famous ghost belongs to ex-Prime Minister Ben Chifley who chose to live at the Hotel Kurrajong in Barton instead of living at The Lodge, because he wanted to be known as a working class man.

THE HAUNTING:

Australia’s 16th prime minister, Ben Chifley, lived in his own room for 11 years at the hotel. He suffered a fatal heart attack in his room at the Kurrajong and there's a constant stream of reports, even as recent as the last couple of weeks, coming out of the Kurrajong of people seeing a grey-suited man pointing towards old Parliament House.

SCARE FACTOR:

Medium. The sight of an apparition in your hotel room, even if just harmlessly pointing out the window, would be an unnerving one.



STRANGE OCCURANCES


Many people who have stayed at the hotel have reported seeing a figure they believe to be the ghost of Chifley, walking around in a grey suit and often near various balconies and windows. The Ghost of Ben Chifley is said to still haunt Hotel Kurrajong, people have seen this man walking about the hotel. Even without seeing or hearing anything, this experience is still eerie.

Duntroon House


GHOST OF SOPHIA


ABOUT:

Built in 1833 (which already hints at a spooky aspect) by Robert Campbell, Duntroon House is the oldest building in Canberra. The house has a special place in the Australian Army’s heritage as it served many purposes, but is now used as the Officer’s Mess at the Royal Military College. But, with it’s rich history comes a haunted one, too.

THE HAUNTING:

The ghost that resides here is one of Canberra’s most well known ones, believed to be Sophia Susanna Campbell, granddaughter of Robert Campbell, who built the house back in 1833. This room, on the first floor, once belonged to Sophia who, on May 31, 1885, died after apparently falling from the room's window, although many still speculate over whether it was accidental or deliberate.

SCARE FACTOR:

High, several top brass of the military refuse to stay in Sophia's former room.



STRANGE OCCURANCES


During the 1970s, college residents began to report glimpses of a glowing ghost of a young woman in 19th century period costume. Soon after, some residents also complained that a bed, freshly made in the morning, would be found in as if it had been slept in later in the day, with pillows hurled around the room. Her presence is very active at this site, with numerous reports of sightings and activity believed to be from Sophia, such as a messy room and ruffled bed. She has been seen in the very room that is speculated on, through hallways, and wandering in the gardens.
